Reaching New Heights: My Epic First Mountain Hike in Nepal at 12,500 Feet!

Why hike in Nepal?

Are you craving a true escape from the daily grind? Yearning for an adventure that immerses you in untouched nature, far from the distractions of modern life? Discover Nepal, where you can hike majestic mountains, connect with welcoming locals, and explore a world like no other.

Ascending to Pathivara: A Tale of Adventure and Faith

At 3 AM, we set out on our hike up the 12,500 ft. peak in Taplejung, Nepal. This journey was not just a hike for us; it was a spiritual quest to reach the Pathivara Temple at the mountain's summit. The Nepalese people believe that any wish made at this temple will be granted by the mountain goddess. Accompanying us were my 7-year-old nephew, Dishant, and his mother, all determined to complete this challenging trek.
Dishant grew tired within the first few hundred feet of our journey. Since we were already hours away from home, turning back was not an option. Fortunately, we found numerous porters, incredibly strong individuals who agreed to carry Dishant for a small fee.



I've experienced several hikes before, from Shenandoah National Park in Virginia to Mount Rainier in Seattle. However, this hike in Nepal, which takes 6 hours to ascend and 3-4 hours to descend, was unlike any other. I had never attempted such a long hike (10 hours in a day) or trekked at such a high elevation.

The scenery from the hill was breathtaking, with nature's vast wilderness all around us. The trail was extremely steep, more akin to climbing than walking. I felt exhausted, and my heartbeat was intense. After 3-4 hours, I needed to rest for 5 minutes for every 2 minutes of hiking. What kept me going was seeing an 18-year-old porter running along the trail, carrying my 60 lb nephew, and my 50-year-old mother managing fine. The belief that only a sinful person could not reach the top also motivated me. Some people I knew couldn't continue and had to turn back without reaching the summit.



Fortunately, all my family members made it to the top. We worshipped the mountain goddess, whose majestic statue felt alive, and each of us made a wish. The reward after such hard work was unforgettable. This beautiful adventure is a memory we will cherish forever.
